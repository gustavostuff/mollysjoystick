DIY Arcade box

Tired of those bulky packages occupying a lot of space? Want a high-quality, small enough joystick that fits perfectly on your desktop so you can play some games instead of working? Are you also a DIY enthusiastic?

Molly's Joystick is compatible with at least 6 different devices:

  • Raspberry Pi Zero 1 and 2
  • Banana Pi M2 Zero
  • Radxa Zero
  • 2 different SJ@JX USB encoders: This one 1 and this one

So it can be either an all-in-one arcade box or a wired USB joystick.

Get the SVG files below, they're free to use 2

A few pictures for the Micro model

Size: 188 x 115 x 57mm

Not small enough you say? Try the Nano size!

Size: 152 x 94 x 57mm

Number of buttons: 6

Materials:

  • A 300x400x3mm acrylic/PETG sheet for Micro (300x300x3mm for Nano)
  • 4 M4 screws, 22mm in length
  • 6 M4 screws, 60mm in length
  • 6 M4 nuts
  • 10 M4 nuts (thin ones, about 2mm in thickness)
  • 4 M2.5 screws and nuts
  • 4-to-6 rubber pads, at least 3mm high (4mm recommended)

Some final notes

  • Plywood may also work, just be carefull when tightening screws
  • As for now, this is specifically designed for JLF TP 8YT Sanwa sticks and 24mm buttons (original ones or copies)
  • SVG files use a 0.5mm stroke width, something like 0.05mm may be needed for an actual laser cutting service
  • Sheet sizes are just the recommended (minimum) ones, since those are the actual document sizes in the files
  • While Micro is easier to use on top of a table, Nano may feel better as a handheld controller, play around and see what is best for you!
