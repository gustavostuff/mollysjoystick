DIY Arcade box

Tired of those bulky boxes occupying a lot of space? Want a high-quality, small enough joystick that fits perfectly on your desktop so you can play some games instead of working? Are you also a DIY enthusiastic? Then this is for you!

Molly's Joystick is compatible with 6 different devices, so it can be either an all-in-one arcade box or USB joystick:

  • Raspberry Pi Zero 1 and 2
  • Banana Pi M2 Zero
  • Radxa Zero
  • 2 different SJ@JX USB encoders: This one1 and this one

A few pictures for the MICRO model, 8 buttons. Cut parts, once assembled, are only 188 x 115 x 57mm (these are prototypes)

Not small enough you say? Try the Nano model! 6 buttons, only 152 x 94 x 57mm

SVG files are free to use under CC 4.0 NC

Materials:

  • A 300x400x3mm acrylic/PETG sheet for MICRO (300x300x3mm for NANO)
  • 4 M4 screws, 22mm in length
  • 4 M4 nuts (thin ones, about 2mm in thickness)
  • 6 M4 screws, 60mm in length
  • 4 M2.5 screws and nuts
  • 4-to-6 rubber pads, at least 3mm high (4mm recommended)

Some final notes

1 compatible only with MICRO
  • Plywood may also work, just be carefull when tightening screws
  • This is specifically designed for (either original or copies) a JLF TP 8YT Sanwa stick and 24mm buttons
  • SVG files use a 0.5mm stroke width, something like 0.05mm may be needed for an actual laser cutting service
  • Sheet sizes are just the recommended (minimum) ones, since that's the actual doc size in the files
  • Round-head screws are recommended
  • While MICRO is easier to use on top of a table, NANO may feel better as a handheld controller, play around and see what is best for you!
