DIY Arcade joystick design

Tired of those bulky Arcade boxes occupying a lot of space? Want a high-quality, small enough joystick that fits perfectly on your desktop so you can play some games instead of working? Are you also a DIY enthusiastic? Then this is for you!

A few pictures for the Micro model (8 buttons). Cut parts, once assembled, are only 188 x 115 x 57mm (these are just some protorypes)

Not small enough you say? Try the Nano model! Only 152 x 94 x 57mm

SVG files are free to use under CC 4.0 NC

Materials:

  • A 300x400x3mm acrylic/PETG sheet for the MICRO size (300x300x3mm for NANO)
  • 4 M4 screws, 22mm in length
  • 4 M4 nuts (thin ones, about 2mm in thickness)
  • 6 M4 screws, 60mm in length (same as the 22mm)
  • 6 M4 nuts (like these)
  • 4 M2.5 screws and nuts same seller as this ones
  • 4-6 rubber pads, at least at least 3mm high (4mm recommended)

Some final notes

  • Plywood may also work but it's more fragile.
  • This is specifically designed for Sanwa parts (either original or copy), and only for 24mm buttons.
  • SVG files use a 0.5mm stroke width, something like 0.05mm may be needed for an actual laser cutting service.
  • Sheet sizes are just the recommended (minimum) ones.
